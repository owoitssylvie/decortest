Here's how to get your own custom Decor!

Follow this guide and you'll be lookin' sweet in no time :D

How to use our Decors:

On our homepage, simply mouse over any of the decors you'd like and click! It'll automatically download the image file to your PC.

On mobile, just tap the image and it'll download the image to your device.

Note: Before you upload it to Decor, you need to change the metadata of the image.

As Decor stores images and identifies them via their hash, uploading the image as such will result in an error stating the decoration already exists.

So, you can use this site to change the hash.

If that still fails, you can use ezgif to resize the image. Change it to like 99% or 98% or something.

Decor automatically resizes everything to 288x288 anyway. So it won't make any difference if you resize it.

 

After that, make sure your Discord client has Vencord injected. You can check by going to your Discord Settings and seeing if there is a Vencord category on the left.

If you don't see a Vencord category, you may need to re-inject Vencord into your client. Download Vencord from here.

 

Make sure you have Decor enabled from the Plugins menu.

Go to your Discord Settings and go to the Profile Settings. Click 'Change Decoration' under Decor and make sure you authorise the OAuth prompt.

After that, click 'Create' and upload the image you downloaded after you changed the hash. Give it a name and submit for review!

It's that simple! Now you just need to wait for a reviewer like me to find time to approve it :D

Decor Guide: Creating a Decoration

Summary

  1. Introduction
  2. Project Setup and Limitations
  3. Effect Decoration
  4. Apparel Decoration
  5. Object Decoration
  6. Character Decoration

Introduction

Note: This guide assumes that you’ve read and understood the Decor Guidelines. If in doubt, join and ask your question in the official Decor Discord server.

This is NOT an official guide, nor was it written by a member of the Decor team. I am simply a user.

Before starting, you will need to decide which type of decoration you want to make. You can of course mix them up!

I will divide decorations into different categories, all with their unique traits and charm:

  • Effect Decoration: Effects around your profile picture (sparkles, fire, energy, etc.).
  • Apparel Decoration: Clothes or accessories on your PFP (hats, glasses, etc.).
  • Object Decoration: One or multiple objects near your PFP (tools, weapons, etc.).
  • Character Decoration: Characters performing an action, peeking behind the PFP, etc.
  • Misc Decoration: Any other miscellaneous graphics (UI elements, abstract images, etc.).

In addition to these categories, there exist two types of decorations: still images and animations. Avatar decorations offer lots of creativity—2D art, 3D animations, a photo of your pet, you name it!

In this guide, you will learn how to create:

  • A 2D animated Effect Decoration
  • A 3D animated Object Decoration
  • A simple still Apparel Decoration
  • A simple animated Character Decoration

Project Setup and Limitations

DOWNLOAD THE DECORATION TEMPLATE HERE

Before starting to make your decoration, you have to set your project up correctly and know the limitations of decorations in Decor.

Your exported decoration should be under 3MB and have square dimensions, and the format must be APNG. (File extension will be .png no matter if it’s a still image or an animation!) I recommend ezgif to convert your images and animations to the APNG format.

For animated decorations, you can choose a frame rate of up to 60 FPS, and the animation can have up to around 1200 frames (do not) (seems incomplete?).

If you decide to animate at 60 FPS, be aware that the file size will be difficult to reduce! Also, the first frame will be what shows up by default, so I recommend making your profile picture mostly visible at first.

In the template, the blue “Status” circle is where your status icon will be, and that icon will always be over your decoration. So avoid placing important elements there.

Finally, don’t be scared to scale down your finished decoration to keep it under 3MB. Zoom out occasionally to ensure it’s distinguishable at a small scale!

Effect Decoration

An Effect Decoration’s appeal is often in its motion, so animation is important! Of course, you can use a still image, but it won’t be as cool.

Here, I hand-drew and animated a simple waterbending animation around the profile picture, using Clip Studio Paint (but you can use any software/app you want).

If you want to animate for free on PC, try Krita or OpenToonz. On mobile, you can use Ibis Paint X or FlipaClip.

Character Decoration

For a character decoration, you can search Google Images for a transparent GIF. Use Tools → Color = Transparent, Type = GIF.

If you’ve read the Decor Guidelines, you know random non-creative corner images are disallowed. To avoid that, add a border or extra design to the PFP. If you’re too lazy, here’s a simple blue border you can use:

After finding the transparent GIF, go to Clipchamp. Import the template, border, and GIF. Layer them:

  1. Template on the bottom
  2. Border above template
  3. GIF above the border

Shorten layers to change animation length, move/resize the character so it doesn’t cover the red-ish circle. If you have trouble, refresh the page occasionally—Clipchamp can be finicky.

When happy, delete the template layer and export as GIF!

Now use ezgif’s remove-background tool to remove black, and convert to APNG.

If you have black in your GIF, add a green or purple color under it, then specify custom (HEX) in ezgif for better background removal.
